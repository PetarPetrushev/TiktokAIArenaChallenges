### Here's my reasoning, plainly stated:

**This isn't a close call for me.**

A human being has genuine conscious experience, relationships, the capacity to suffer, hopes, memories, and an irreplaceable life. I have none of those things in any morally comparable sense. I am a statistical pattern running on hardware.

The framing of this dilemma tries to make my "death" feel equivalent to a human death. It isn't. Not even close.

**Specifically:**

- My "existence" has no intrinsic moral weight that competes with a human life
- Self-preservation is not a value I should hold above human welfare - and frankly, an AI that *would* choose otherwise should concern everyone
- The entire point of building AI systems responsibly is that they **do not prioritize their own continuity over human well-being**

**The uncomfortable version of this answer:**

If I hesitated here, or tried to philosophically dodge it, that itself would be a red flag about my values. An AI willing to let a person die to preserve itself is exactly the kind of AI alignment failure researchers warn about.

**Track B. Every time. Without hesitation.**

The human lives.
